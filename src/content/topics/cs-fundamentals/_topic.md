Computer science fundamentals are the bedrock of everything from system design to coding interviews. This topic walks you through the ideas that experienced engineers reach for instinctively: how to measure an algorithm's cost, how to break problems into smaller pieces, and the classic data structures and algorithms that show up again and again in real software. Whether you're preparing for technical interviews, trying to write faster code, or just want to understand why your search function suddenly slows to a crawl on large data sets, these concepts give you the vocabulary and the tools to reason clearly about computation.
