Frontend engineering at the senior level is not just writing components — it is owning the quality, reliability, and longevity of a user-facing system. That means writing TypeScript that catches whole classes of bugs at compile time, testing strategies that give you confidence without becoming a maintenance burden, performance work that keeps interactions snappy on real devices in the real world, and accessibility practices that make your product usable by everyone. It also means understanding how your code gets bundled and deployed, designing systems that can be extended without breaking, and securing the client layer against the most common attack vectors. This topic covers the cross-cutting concerns that distinguish a senior frontend engineer from a developer who simply knows a framework.
